This follows on from the post of:

http://thread.gmane.org/gmane.science.nmr.relax.devel/3882

Now in relax, the 'No Rex', 'LM63', and 'CR72' models for CPMG-type
experiments - respectively no chemical exchange, the Luz Meiboom 1963,
and the Carver and Richards 1972 models - can be optimised
successfully.  Below is a comparison of the results from relax, NESSY,
CPMGFit, and ShereKhan.  The NESSY chi-squared values are smaller most
likely due to a difference in how peak intensity errors are handled
within the program.  To note:  relax and ShereKhan, at least for these
models, consistently produce the same results with slightly better
accuracy than CPMGFit.

Regards,

Edward

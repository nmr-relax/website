Hi,

One trick that might help is to replace the hymbolic trigonometric
functions.  cosh(x) can be replaced by cos(ix) or exponential
functions.  But I've never found sympy to work well.  For example the
number of 0.353553390593274 that appears a few times really worries
me.  Anyway, I would highly recommend that you try out the very old
(1982) and free wxMaxima software:

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maxima_%28software%29
https://andrejv.github.io/wxmaxima/
http://maxima.sourceforge.net/

This is very powerful software and it will handle the CR72 equation
partial derivatives much better.  This originates from Macsyma
(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macsyma), and Mathematica took many of
it's ideas from it.  Mathematica also works very well because of the
powerful FullSimplify[] function that Maxima does not have.  I have
used Maxima for most of the symbolic equation derivations that are in
relax.  Anyway, you can save the maxima text files in the relax source
directories alongside the sympy scripts.
